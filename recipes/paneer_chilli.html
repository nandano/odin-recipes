Paneer Chilli

Chilli Paneer is a vegetarian version of Chilli Chicken and has become one of the most sought after dishes in Indo-Chinese cuisine. I have been told that it tastes similar to General Tso’s recipe, though it is made with Indian cottage cheese instead of tofu.

Ingredients

  • Paneer (Cottage Cheeze)
  • Maida
  • Ginger garlic paste
  • Black Pepper
  • Kashmiri Chilli Powder
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Green Chilli
  • Chopped spring onions
  • Capsicum
  • Soy Sauce
  • Red chilli sauce
  • Cornstarch

Steps

  1. In a bowl take 2 tablespoons cornstarch and 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida).
  2. Next add ½ teaspoon ginger paste and ½ teaspoon garlic paste.
  3. Add ¼ teaspoon crushed black pepper or black pepper powder and ¼ teaspoon Kashmiri red chilli powder. Season with salt to taste.
  4. Next add ¼ cup water or add as required. With a wired whisk or a spoon mix very well to get a smooth batter without any lumps. The batter is of medium consistency.
  5. Add paneer cubes in the batter. With a spoon, gently coat the paneer cubes with the batter.
  6. Fry these cubes of paneer in a frying pan
  7. In the same pan, add 2 teaspoons finely chopped ginger, 2 teaspoons finely chopped garlic and 2 to 3 green chillies (slit or sliced).
  8. Stir fry on medium to high heat for about 30 seconds or until fragrant.
  9. Then add ⅓ cup chopped spring onions (scallions). Stir fry spring onions for 1 to 2 minutes on medium to high heat.
  10. Next add ½ cup sliced capsicum.
  11. Stir fry on medium to high heat for 3 to 4 minutes. The capsicum only needs to slightly cooked.
  12. Lower the heat and then add ½ teaspoon Kashmiri red chilli powder or sweet paprika.
  13. Then add 2 teaspoons soy sauce.
  14. Next add 1 teaspoon red chilli sauce. The red chilli sauce has to be spicy.
  15. Stir and mix the sauces and red chilli powder with the remaining stir fried ingredients.
  16. Add ½ to ⅔ cup water.
  17. Mix well.
  18. Let the chilli sauce mixture come to a simmer on medium to medium-high heat.
  19. Add cornstarch and maida and mix well to make a fine paste without any lumps and add this paste to the sauce
  20. Season with salt to taste. Be sure to taste the sauce first as soy sauce and red chilli sauce already have salt in them.
  21. Mix very well and simmer till the sauce has slightly thickened and the cornstarch has cooked.
